1992

In Point Breeze, Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania, on January 19, 1992, Malcom James McCormick was born. His mother, Karen Meyers, a photographer, and father Mark McCormick an architect. He had an older brother named Miller.

2007

Easy Mac With the Cheesy Raps

Going by his first, original stage name of Easy Mac at the age of fifteen, he released his first mix tape But My Mackin’ Ain’t Easy.

2009

It Ain’t 2009 No More

Established himself as Mac Miller by releasing two more mixtapes: The Jukebox: Prelude to Class Clown and The High Life.

2010

Mac won the 21 & Under of the Year award as well as the Best Hip Hop Video for “Live Free” at the Pittsburgh Hip Hop Awards.

2010

Signed his first record label with Rostrum Records as a result of his mixtape K.I.D.S.

2011

Mac Miller earned his place amongst other big name rappers on the “Freshmen Class” list published by XXL magazine.

2011

Blue Slide Park, Mac’s debut studio album released on November 8th. This album made records as it sold 144,000 copies in its first week of sales and made its way to the top of the Billboard 200.

2012

His seventh mixtape, Macadelic, was released on March, 23rd.

2013

REMeMber

In honor of a friend he lost, Miller founded the record label REMember Music whose primary focus was on artists in his hometown of Pittsburgh. He also used this record label to release songs under his various other alter-ego’s including Larry Lovestein and Delusional Thomas.

2013

The Best Day Ever

September 20th was declared “Mac Miller Day” by Pittsburgh mayor Luke Ravenstahl who presented Mac with a key to the city.

2014

Mac moved on from Rostrum Records and signed a recording contract and distribution deal for REMember Music with Warner Bros. Records, but not before independently releasing his tenth solo mixtape Faces.

2015

Mac Miller released his major label debut album GO:OD AM on September 18th. It reached the number four spot on the Billboard 200 and the album along with its single “Weekend”, were certified gold and platinum by the RIAA.

2016

Taking on an exploration of love, singing, R&B, jazz, and funk, The Divine Feminine was released on September 16th. This album debuted at number two on the Billboard 200 and number one on Billboard's Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums.

2018

Swimming, released on August 3rd, was his fifth and what became his last studio album. This was also his fifth consecutive top-five charting album on the Billboard 200.

2018

On September 7th at the age of 26, Malcom McCormick passed of an accidental drug overdose. This was just before his Swimming tour was set to begin.
